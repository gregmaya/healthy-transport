Healthy Transport
Score mode

Contextual — weights each street by the actual age mix of nearby residents. Elderly, children, and working-age adults each have different optimal walk distances. Reveals where a stop benefits the most people given today's population.

Baseline — scores streets on network reach alone: how many address points fall within walking distance, every resident counted equally. Useful for identifying locations with broad physical reach, independent of today's demographics.

Weighted by who lives nearby — age-disaggregated, modelled estimates.

Infrastructure potential — shortest paths to all address points, equal weight per resident.

Demographic group

Each group uses a different walk-distance curve. Working-age adults tolerate longer walks (peak ~500m); elderly residents drop off sharply beyond 300m; children's benefit falls to zero around 600m. Scores show how much health benefit a stop at that segment would generate for that group specifically.

Population data is modelled from dwelling type and neighbourhood census — not individual-level data.

Overlays

Bus stops — existing stop locations from the national GTFS feed (Rejseplanen). Internal stops are coloured by their score; use them to compare current provision against high-benefit segments.

Demographics — population density heatmap built from modelled entrance-level estimates. Grey = sparse; golden yellow = dense residential core. Switch demographic group to update the heatmap weight.
